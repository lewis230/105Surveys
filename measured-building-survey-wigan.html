Local Knowledge

Wigan's Victorian and Industrial Stock: What the Survey Needs to Capture

Wigan's built environment is dominated by two overlapping typologies: Victorian commercial and civic buildings in the town centre, and former mill and industrial buildings in the surrounding boroughs of Leigh, Atherton and Hindley. Both are generating active conversion and refurbishment pipelines. Town centre commercial buildings on Standishgate and Wallgate are being brought forward for change of use and upper-floor residential conversion. Former mill buildings in Leigh and Atherton are seeing residential conversion proposals at an increasing rate.

Mill buildings present a specific survey challenge: structural bays at 3 to 5 metre centres, full-height internal volumes with inserted mezzanine floors, and external envelope details — window openings, brickwork bond, chimney stacks — that need accurate recording for heritage and planning purposes. Our laser scanning approach captures full point cloud data of the building interior and exterior in a single site visit, producing drawings that cover every level and elevation without the need for a return visit.

Wigan Council requires accurate existing condition drawings for planning applications involving works to existing buildings, including schemes within the Wigan town centre conservation area. Standishgate and the Market Place area are subject to conservation area designation, and planning applications for these properties are assessed with additional scrutiny. We ensure every measured building survey delivered in Wigan meets the council's validation requirements first time.
