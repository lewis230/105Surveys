## What a Measured Building Survey Actually Gives You

A measured building survey captures the precise dimensions and layout of an existing building — floor plans, elevations, and sections — and delivers them as accurate CAD drawings you can design from directly.

Without it, you're working from assumptions. Approximate dimensions from a previous planning application. Sketches from a site visit. An old set of drawings that may or may not reflect what's actually there.

Every one of those introduces risk. A wall that's 150mm thicker than you thought. A ceiling height that doesn't work with your proposed scheme. A structural element that wasn't shown on the original drawings.

A measured building survey removes that uncertainty before it becomes a problem.

## Why Timing Matters

The survey is almost always on the critical path at the start of a project. If it comes in late, your design programme slips. If the data is inaccurate, you're back on site.

## What's Included in the Survey Drawings

A standard measured building survey from 105 Surveys includes:

- **Floor plans** at each level
- **External elevations** of all principal façades
- **Cross-sections** through the building showing floor-to-ceiling heights
- **Structural features**, door and window positions and sizes
- All supplied in AutoCAD (.dwg), PDF, or your preferred format
